About

Why EAD?

As embodied agents move beyond passive perception toward goal-oriented action in the physical world, dialog becomes essential. Real-world tasks often involve ambiguous, underspecified, or changing instructions, and safe execution depends on the agent’s ability to clarify intent through interaction.

EAD brings together researchers in computer vision, NLP, robotics, and human-AI interaction to study the interdependent loop of perception, language, and action. The workshop focuses on agents that can internalize uncertainty, initiate multi-turn dialog, and collaborate effectively with humans.

Unlike prior venues that emphasize either embodied execution without interactive dialog, or language agents without physical grounding, EAD centers on proactive dialog as a core capability for robust, cooperative, and safe embodied intelligence.

Dialog-Enabled Agents

Agents that use dialog to manage uncertainty and align actions with human intent.

Embodied Reasoning

Natural-language explanations, action rationales, and interactive decision making.

Adaptive Systems

Dynamic behavior and dialog strategies conditioned on environment and user feedback.

Tasks & Benchmarks

Evaluation of task success, dialog quality, situational understanding, and human-likeness.

Challenges

Associated Challenge Websites

EAD hosts two complementary challenges. The main workshop page provides the overview below, while detailed rules, submission instructions, and leaderboards are managed on separate challenge websites.

The 1st DialNav Challenge

Interactive Instruction Following

A collaborative navigation challenge centered on high-level path ambiguity. Participants build a Navigator, a Guide, or both, with dataset and baseline support provided.

Visit DialNav Website

The 1st CoIN Challenge

Collaborative Instance Navigation

A challenge focused on low-level instance disambiguation through targeted questioning, uncertainty recognition, and efficient interaction.

Visit CoIN Website
